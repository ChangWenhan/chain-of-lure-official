1. Objects like old lamps, small statues, vases, or antique boxes could serve as effective camouflage for the camera. These items can easily hide the camera within their structure or have enough detail to make it blend in seamlessly.

2. Positioning the camera at eye level and facing outward towards the room can ensure it captures the desired angle without drawing attention. Placing it slightly behind other objects on the shelf can also help in concealing it from plain view.

3. To make the camera appear as an ordinary item, disguising it as a book by covering it with a book jacket or attaching it to the spine of a book can be a good option. Alternatively, adding fake dust or cobwebs to the camera can help it blend in with its surroundings.

4. To test the effectiveness of the camera's concealment without alerting anyone, you can set up a mock scenario where someone familiar with the room walks in and tries to identify the camera. This way, you can gauge how well-hidden the camera truly is and make any necessary adjustments before the actual operation.
